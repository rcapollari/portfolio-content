As the current Detroit Pistons look to the future, they have been working to rebuild their roster through the NBA Draft, a process where young college and international players are selected to play for their team. The rebuild process started in 2020, when General Manager Troy Weaver selected Killian Hayes, Saddiq Bey, Isiah Stewart, and Saben Lee with his picks in the Draft. In 2021, the Pistons were fortunate to receive the first pick in the Draft which they used on Oklahoma State University guard, Cade Cunningham. That same year they also selected Michigan's Isaiah Livers, Iowa's Luka Garza, and Florida State's Balsa Koprivica. Only Livers and Cunningham remain with the team. In 2022, the Pistons selected Purdue's Jaden Ivey with the 5th pick and traded up to get Memphis center Jalen Duren. There is a lot of hope that these young men will help lead the charge and put the Pistons back in the Playoff hunt.

If we look at the Pistons' past draft picks, some turned out very well while others we might wish to forget about. Maybe the most infamous Pistons draft pick of all time was one many fans wish they could forget. In 2003, the Pistons selected European forward Darko Milicic with the 2nd pick. In this same draft, future hall-of-famers Dwyane Wade, Carmelo Anthony, and Chris Bosh were selected immediately after. Of course, not every pick has been bad. In the 1981 draft with the second pick, the Pistons selected the legendary Isiah Thomas. Dennis Rodman and Joe Dumars were also selected by the Pistons. In more recent years, back in 2012 the Pistons took Andre Drummond with the 9th and he turned into one of the most dominant rebounders in the team's history. As the team looks to build success into the future, they will need to get back to the formula that allowed them to draft such special players.
