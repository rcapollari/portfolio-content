The Pistons have 3 NBA championships, which they won in 1989, 1990, and 2004. Only 8 NBA franchises have 3 or more championships. The Pistons' first two titles were won off the backs of the self-proclaimed "Bad Boys," a team that played hard-nosed defense and weren't afraid to get physical. They were led by a hall-of-fame backcourt with Isiah Thomas and Joe Dumars. Rick Mahorn and Bill Laimbeer were the enforcers in the frontcourt, and Dennis Rodman was the pest on the boards. This team is highly credited with ending the Los Angeles Lakers' dynasty of the 1980s.

2004 was the year of the "Going to Work" Pistons team that embodied the same defensive prowess of the Pistons championship teams of the past. This team was special because none of their players had ever made an all-star game up to that point. Led by coach Larry Brown, a starting lineup of Chauncey Billups, Richard "Rip" Hamilton, Tayshaun Prince, Rasheed Wallace, and Ben Wallace took the NBA by storm when they beat the powerhouse Los Angeles Lakers once again in the NBA Finals. The Lakers, who were led by future hall-of-famers Shaquille O'Neal and Kobe Bryant, were heavily favored to win this series. Instead the Pistons took the trophy home within 5 games of a 7-game series.
